New Grant Awarded for AI Research

Our laboratory is proud to announce that we have received a $2.5 million grant from the National Science Foundation to develop new AI algorithms for healthcare applications. This substantial funding will support our research team's efforts to create innovative machine learning models that can improve disease diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient outcome prediction.

Led by Prof. Sarah Johnson, the three-year project titled "Privacy-Preserving AI for Clinical Decision Support" will focus on developing algorithms that can learn from sensitive medical data while maintaining strict privacy guarantees for patient information. The research will combine differential privacy techniques with federated learning approaches to enable collaboration across multiple healthcare institutions without compromising data confidentiality.

"This grant provides us with the resources needed to tackle one of the most significant challenges in healthcare AI - how to leverage vast amounts of medical data for improved patient care while ensuring the privacy and security of sensitive information," said Prof. Johnson. "We're excited to push the boundaries of what's possible in this important field."

The project will involve collaboration with three major medical centers and will provide funding for two postdoctoral researchers and four graduate students. The team expects to begin work immediately, with initial results anticipated by the end of the year.

Research Objectives

  • Develop privacy-preserving machine learning algorithms specifically tailored for healthcare applications
  • Create secure federated learning frameworks that can operate across institutional boundaries
  • Implement and validate the approaches on real-world medical datasets
  • Establish formal privacy guarantees and security analysis for all developed techniques

The grant also includes funding for outreach activities, including workshops and courses to help healthcare professionals understand and implement AI techniques in their work. The team plans to make all developed algorithms available as open-source software to maximize the impact of the research.
